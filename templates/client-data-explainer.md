## The short version

We use generative AI tools (including Claude by Anthropic) to assist with document review, research, drafting, and routine workflows on our matters. Every AI-assisted work product is supervised by an attorney, every citation we include is independently verified, and AI does not change our professional responsibility to you.

We use commercial-grade AI tools that do not train on client data. We do not use free or consumer-tier AI for any work involving your information.

If you have concerns or want specific restrictions on how AI is used on your matter, talk to the partner responsible for your engagement. We will accommodate reasonable requests and document them in our matter file.

## What AI does on your matter

When we use AI on your work, it typically does one or more of these things:

- **Reviews documents** — contracts, filings, transcripts, regulatory text — and surfaces what we should pay attention to
- **Drafts first passes** — memos, briefs, correspondence — that an attorney edits before delivery
- **Researches legal questions** — finds relevant case law, statutes, and regulations from grounded legal-research databases
- **Compiles internal summaries** — status updates, deal points, matter histories — for our team's use
- **Tracks routine workflows** — deadlines, intake, scheduling — to free our attorneys for substantive work

In every case, an attorney reviews and is responsible for the output.

## What AI does not do on your matter

- **AI does not make legal judgments for you.** When we apply law to your facts, that is our work, not the AI's. AI accelerates the routine; the judgment is human.
- **AI does not replace attorney supervision.** A licensed attorney reviews, edits, and signs off on every AI-assisted output before it leaves our firm.
- **AI does not see data outside our firm-controlled environment.** We do not paste your information into free or personal AI accounts. All AI use is on enterprise-grade tools authenticated to firm-issued accounts.
- **AI does not train on your data.** Our contractual agreements with AI vendors prohibit them from using your information to improve their models.

## How we keep your data safe

Four things have to be true at the same time for AI use to be privilege-safe. Our firm meets all four:

1. **Vendor agreement** — Our AI vendors agree in writing not to train on client data. Their privacy and security commitments are independently audited (SOC 2 Type II).
2. **Vendor infrastructure** — Their systems are encrypted, access-controlled, and operate within data-residency regions appropriate to your matter.
3. **Firm configuration** — Internally, we restrict AI access to the lawyers and staff working on your matter. Sensitive actions (sending email, modifying documents) require attorney approval before execution.
4. **Per-matter scoping** — Each matter has its own AI workspace. We do not mix data across matters or clients.

Underpinning all four: every member of our firm using AI has completed our firm's AI training and is bound by our internal AI policy.

## Citations and accuracy

You may have read about court filings containing fabricated case citations. Those incidents typically share a pattern: someone used AI without verifying the output before filing.

Our verification practice:

- **Every citation we include in a filing or memo is independently verified** to exist as cited
- **Every quoted phrase is verified to appear verbatim** in the source we cite it from
- **Every "the court held" or "the statute provides" assertion is verified** against the underlying source

We use AI tooling to help with this verification (a citation-verifier skill that round-trips quotes against sources), but a lawyer reads and approves the result. The verification is not automated; it is AI-assisted.

## Your control

You decide how AI is used on your matter:

- **Standard:** We use AI per the above standards, with attorney supervision and verification on every deliverable.
- **Restricted:** If you prefer no AI use on your matter, or AI use only for specific tasks (e.g., research only, no drafting), tell us and we will document it in our matter file.
- **Vendor-restricted:** If you prefer specific AI vendors not be used (e.g., only certain providers, only US-based providers), we can accommodate this depending on the matter's requirements.

There is no penalty for asking us to limit or exclude AI on your matter. We will discuss tradeoffs (some restrictions affect the speed and cost of work) and find an arrangement that fits your needs.

## Our responsibility to you

The American Bar Association's Model Rules require lawyers to provide competent representation, maintain client confidentiality, and supervise non-lawyer assistants. AI does not change those obligations — it operates under them.

If we err in our use of AI on your matter — a missed verification, a configuration mistake, a vendor incident — we treat that as a professional responsibility issue, not a tooling issue. The accountability is ours.
